Historical Information

When you think about Draper, you probably don't think about pioneers. Draper is generally thought of for it's proximity to the mountain, the hiking and biking trails, and of course, the temple on the mountain. It wasn't alway that way. It was the mountains and especially the mountain runoff that in 1849 first attracted Ebenezer Brown to "South Willow Creek" to fatten his cattle on the long grasses growing in the area. After the first year was successful he invited his brother in law, William Draper III and his family to the area. In the next two years another 20 family moved to the area and it was officially called "Draperville" in honor of their first presiding elder, William Draper III. Over the next few years the settles started to have troubles with the indigenous people and a fort was erected called "Fort Draper". At the site of where Fort Draper once stood is now a historic park, honoring the original settlers and their resiliance in difficult times.
